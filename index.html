What Do They Mostly Eat?

These little carnivores are mostly insectivorous in the wild, eating just about any kind of meaty delicacy that comes their way. Moreover, the insect larvae. Usually kept as pets, they will gladly eat delectable foods like bloodworms, brine shrimp, and commercial pellets made with appropriate proteins.

Some pet owners like incorporating sporadic treats of land meat or vegetables, including chicken or peas. Since these fish are not designed to eat anything coming from outside the water, this may get fairly contentious at times.

Homemade Food for The Betta

When you don't feel like going to the shop, a simple paste might be a terrific alternative lunch. A little bit of chicken liver, a dash of garlic (or garlic sauce), and just a few minutes of your time are all that is required. Simply combine the two and process until homogeneous paste forms.

Utilizing a syringe is the simplest method to feed the betta this paste without creating a huge mess of food remnants in the tank. Just inject a small amount into the syringe, allowing the top to touch the tank.

Since bettas like to eat close to the top of the tank, it is not necessary to reach too far within. Just let the paste flow out when your pet is around, then hold it for a moment before discharging it once again.

Benefits Of the Garlic Paste

Small chicken liver snacks can increase the number of good proteins in your betta's diet without reducing their intake of fat. Additionally, garlic puts a lot of effort into enhancing its immune response.

Garlic undoubtedly offers advantages because it is occasionally advised to add it to the pellet if fish possess internal parasites or even fungi. And maybe most significantly, bettas appear to enjoy it a lot!

Feeding your betta solely paste is not a good idea since they enjoy eating entire things like bloodworms and pellets. Bettas may chew on these items for a long time with their small teeth, keeping them occupied and active.

There is absolutely nothing to be concerned about if the pet goes without food for a few days while you are away. Additionally, some pet owners even enjoy fasting their animals for one day every so often, which is said to be advantageous.
